## Crackpots

The physics community seems to attract a large number of crackpots, people outside the community that seems to think the current physics establishment (if not entire academic) is nonsense or going down the wrong path and that they themselves (whether they have been trained in physics or not) have valid "theories" that should be considered. For some reason, it's often a theory of everything. The above should not be taken as a definition (they can fulfill all of the above or just a few), but it is easy to recognize a crackpot when they appear. 

The good faith ones oftentimes try to get physicists to take a look at their work, even if it's nonsense. My colleagues and professors get emails and paper mail way too often. When I was an *undergrad* and had a paper go onto the arxiv, I received several emails. 

Sometimes these crackpots can be harmless; Chris Pope once told me he got an email saying "I'm building a black hole in my garage, should I be worried about anything?" These types are fine and often teachable. 

Other times, they can be somewhat concerning. We have had our fair share of crackpots walk onto the theory floor and demand to talk to someone about their "work".
